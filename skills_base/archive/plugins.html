HyperTV Macintosh Plugin

ACTV originally developed their HyperTV internet browser plugin for Windows.  They wanted to support their Macintosh client base as well, so they hired me to port the plugin.  This is a C++ module that coordinates a stream of web documents and chat with a video stream.  The original was written for windows using VC++.  I ported it to Mac OS 8, using Codewarrior and Netscape's plug-in framework, and developed the Mac installer using Installer Vise.  I completed work on this project in August, 2000.

Check out www.hypertv.com.

eSchool ActiveX Control

In 1998, ACTV asked me to help them work around a problem they were having using Java with Internet Explorer 5 for Macintosh in their eSchool product.  I developed a Macintosh ActiveX control in C for Internet Explorer that allowed them to coordinate video with a timed stream of web documents.  Check out the eschool site at www.eschoolonline.com.

A Field Trip to the Sky

In 1996, I completed programming of a complex astronomy CD, A Field Trip to the Sky.  Terra Associates originally hired me in 1995 to work on the project, which they developed for SunBurst based on an Apple II product.  I programmed the product entirely in Director, except for the lab section.  For this section I created an XObject in C for both Windows and Macintosh, based on the books Astronomical Algorithms and Elements of Solar Eclipses 1951-2000, by Jean Meuss.  The functions included: rendering a 3D model of the Earth, Sun, and Moon based on the date;  rendering a 3D model of the shadow cast on the Earth and Moon by the sun, depending on the date; rendering a shadow of a person standing on the earth at a given date, time and location (including eclipses).  Boing! LLC took over the project, completing it in 1996.  Buy It!
